ABOUT RADARSONICS

From switching rooms to cloud-native networks.

Radarsonics began life in the mid-1990s as a small specialist team helping operators maintain and upgrade switching systems and transmission networks. Over the years, we have grown into a full-stack partner for telecommunication solutions and information technology — still obsessed with reliability, but now working across physical, virtual and cloud environments.

Founded by Mr. Shreedhar Patil, Radarsonics is built on the belief that a stable, well-run network is the foundation for any digital ambition. As networks, applications and devices have evolved, we have continuously retooled ourselves while staying close to the realities of field operations and enterprise IT.

OUR PHILOSOPHY

Engineering discipline, business clarity, human simplicity.

We apply telecom-grade discipline to every environment we touch, but we also speak the language of business and design experiences that feel simple for users.

Engineering discipline

We treat every network, data centre and cloud environment as a system of systems — mapping dependencies, failure modes and handoffs.

  • Documented architectures and runbooks
  • Standardised patterns and accelerators
  • Clear SLOs and operational metrics

Business clarity

We obsess over the “why” behind every initiative, linking technology decisions to customer experience, revenue and cost outcomes.

  • Joint roadmaps and value cases
  • Dashboards that business teams actually use
  • Transparent progress and risk reporting

Human simplicity

We design processes, portals and tools so that engineers, field technicians and end users can do their jobs with confidence.

  • Operator and user-centric design
  • Continuous feedback from teams in the field
  • Training and enablement as part of every rollout

Partner mindset

We prefer long-term relationships to short-term wins. Many of our clients have worked with us for more than a decade.

  • Shared KPIs and co-owned outcomes
  • Joint innovation roadmaps
  • Transparent governance and communication
